My name is Hang Gao, a PhD student in Mechanical Engineering at Cornell University. I am advised by [Prof. E. Farrell Helbling](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=Pwd7hJYAAAAJ&hl=en) and affiliated with the [Helbling Lab](https://helbling-lab.github.io/). My current research focuses on insect-scale flapping-wing robots, interfacial flight, and micro-robotics.

I am leading the end-to-end development of **GammaBot**, an untethered 961 mg flapping-wing robot capable of high-speed water-surface locomotion, achieving 0.89 m/s forward speed, 400 deg/s turning, and over 10 minutes of endurance on a single charge.

Previously, I received my M.S. in Mechanical Engineering from the University of California San Diego, where I worked with [Prof. Nick Gravish](https://gravishlab.ucsd.edu/) on compliant thorax design for flapping-wing robots.
