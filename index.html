Hang Gao

My name is Hang Gao, a PhD student in Mechanical Engineering at Cornell University. I am advised by Prof. E. Farrell Helbling and affiliated with the Helbling Lab. My current research focuses on the system-level modeling, design, control/estimation, and experimental validation of insect-scale flapping-wing robots operating under strict size, weight, power, and computation (SWPaC) constraints, where performance is fundamentally shaped by scaling laws.

I am leading the end-to-end development of GammaBot, an untethered 961 mg flapping-wing robot capable of high-speed water-surface locomotion, achieving 0.89 m/s forward speed, 400 deg/s turning, and over 10 minutes of endurance on a single charge.

Previously, I received my M.S. in Mechanical Engineering from the University of California San Diego, where I worked with Prof. Nick Gravish on compliant thorax design for flapping-wing robots.
